Step One

Become Street Homeless

A person no longer has a place to stay and begins to live on the street full time.

Overview

People need to live on the streets nine months to meet the city’s “chronicity requirement.” Until reaching that point, and working with outreach teams to prove it, the majority of housing programs, transitional or permanent, are not options.

In order to be eligible for a Safe Haven, a person must meet, and provide a way to prove they meet, the city’s chronicity requirement. This provides a barrier to many people who have recently become homeless or left the shelter system, leading to a wait of up to nine months before becoming eligible for transitional housing. In practice, we have found that people who are working with a case manager (see step 2) are often in limbo; people experiencing street homeless want to move into transitional housing but have to wait for one of two things (sometimes both): they become eligible or a vacancy becomes available. Daiski, I. (2007). Perspectives of homeless people on their health and health needs priorities. Journal of Advanced Nursing.
